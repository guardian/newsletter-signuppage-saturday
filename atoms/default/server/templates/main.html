Election 2020 Scenes from a presidency

Memorable moments from four years of Donald Trump's time in office - in pictures

Captions by David Smith, Design by Ben Longden
  • Share on Facebook

2017

Jan

American carnage

Read more Donald Trump becomes the 45th US president and speaks of ‘American carnage’ in his inaugural address. Former president George W Bush reportedly says afterwards: ‘Well, that was some weird shit.’ Sean Spicer makes his debut as White House press secretary by falsely claiming that the inauguration drew a bigger crowd than Barack Obama’s. Photograph by Jim Lo Scalzo/EPA

2017

Jan

Women on the march

Read more Trump narrowly prevented Hillary Clinton becoming the first female president. A day after his inauguration, an estimated 470,000 people take part in the Women’s March in Washington and an estimated 4 million march in cities across the country. It is described as the biggest single-day demonstration in recorded American history. Photo by Mario Tama/Getty Images

2017

Jan

Travel ban chaos

Read more Trump followed through on a campaign promise of ‘extreme vetting’ by signing an executive order to restrict people from seven Muslim-majority countries from entering the US. The result is chaos and mass protests at airports in Dallas, Chicago, New York and elsewhere, followed by a prolonged legal battle. Photo by Stephanie Keith/Getty Images

2017

May

The orb

Read more In a signal of intent, Trump’s first international trip is to staunch ally Saudi Arabia, where he, Melania Trump, the first lady, King Salman bin Abdulaziz al-Saud and Egypt’s president, Abdel Fatah el-Sisi, place their hands on an illuminated globe during the inauguration ceremony. Photo by Bandar Algaloud / Saudi Royal Council via Getty Images

2017

June

The Comey rule

Read more Fired by Trump, James Comey testifies before the Senate intelligence committee about Russia’s interference in the 2016 election. When the FBI director is asked about a Trump tweet suggesting there could be secret recordings of their conversations, Comey replies: ‘Lordy, I hope there are tapes.’ Photograph by Jonathan Ernst/Reuters

2017

May

We’ll always have Paris

Read more Trump sticks by a campaign pledge when he announces that the US will withdraw from the Paris climate agreement and says: ‘I was elected to represent the citizens of Pittsburgh, not Paris.’ But Bill Peduto, the Democratic mayor of Pittsburgh, responded: “I can assure you that we will follow the guidelines of the Paris agreement for our people, our economy & future.” Photo by Kevin Lamarque/Reuters

2017

July

The Mooch

Read more Anthony Scaramucci delivers a press briefing with swagger and an air kiss to the White House press corps but is at daggers drawn with the chief of staff, Reince Priebus. Scaramucci is out after just 11 days; Priebus follows soon after amid months of extraordinary staff turnover. Photograph by Pablo Martinez Monsivais/AP

2017

Aug

Charlottesville

Read more Several hundred white nationalists marched in Charlottesville, Virginia, chanting: ‘White lives matter! You will not replace us! Jews will not replace us!’ A civil rights protester was killed in subsequent violence. Trump said there ‘were very fine people on both sides’. Photo by Evelyn Hockstein/The Washington Post via Getty Images

2017

Oct

Paper towel-gate

Read more Trump tosses paper towels into a crowd at Calvary Chapel in Guaynabo, Puerto Rico, after Hurricane Maria wreaked destruction in the region. He is criticised for a lacklustre response and clashes bitterly with Carmen Yulín Cruz, the mayor of San Juan. Photo by Evan Vucci/AP

2018

Feb

Parkland

Read more A 19-year-old with an AR-15 semiautomatic rifle opens fire on his former classmates and teachers, killing 17 people and injuring 17 more at Marjory Stoneman Douglas high school in Parkland, Florida – the deadliest high school shooting in American history. Samuel Zeif, a student at the school, weeps as he recalls the tragedy during a listening session at the White House. Photo by Ricky Carioti/The Washington Post via Getty Images

2018

Feb

A new Hope

Read more Hope Hicks, communications director at the White House, leaves the US Capitol after attending a House intelligence committee closed-door meeting. A day later she resigns and joins the Fox Corporation, only to return to Trump’s side in 2020. Photo by Leah Millis/Reuters

2018

Apr

Stormy weather

Read more Stormy Daniels, a pornographic film actor whose real name is Stephanie Clifford, claims that she had a sexual encounter with Trump in 2006 and was paid “hush money” as part of a non-disclosure agreement. She arrives in court in New York for a hearing related to Michael Cohen, Trump’s longtime lawyer, who made the $130,000 payment. Photo by Yana Paskova/Getty Images

2018

Jun

Diplomacy as a Baroque painting

Read more The German chancellor, Angela Merkel, and other world leaders stand over a seated Trump during the G7 summit in Charlevoix, Quebec, in Canada. The picture becomes an internet classic with one tweet suggesting this caption: ‘Leader of the free world meets president of the United States.’ Photo by Jesco Denzel /Bundesregierung via Getty Images

2018

Jun

Zero tolerance

Read more Immigrant children are housed in a tent encampment under a new ‘zero tolerance’ policy by the Trump administration. They are walking in single file at the facility near the Mexican border in Tornillo, Texas. By October 2020, lawyers are still struggling to find the parents of 545 children separated from them, the American Civil Liberties Union says. Photograph by Mike Blake/Reuters

2018

Jul

There’s a bear in the woods

Read more Trump meets Vladimir Putin in Helsinki. He is condemned as “treasonous” for siding with the Kremlin over his own government agencies after he seemingly accepts the Russian president’s denial of election meddling during an extraordinary press conference. Photo by Brendan Smialowski / AFP via Getty Images

2018

Sept

The war over Kavanaugh

Read more Christine Blasey Ford is sworn in before the Senate judiciary committee. In searing testimony, she accuses Judge Brett Kavanaugh of sexually assaulting her when they were teenagers. Kavanaugh denies the allegation with fury and indignation and is confirmed to the supreme court. Erin Schaff/The New York Times via eyevine

2018

Dec

Cohen goes down

Read more Michael Cohen, who once said he would take a bullet for Trump, arrives with daughter Samantha Blake Cohen and wife Laura Shusterman at a US federal court in New York. He is sentenced to three years in prison for campaign finance violations and other crimes but is released on a medical furlough. Photo by Timothy A. Clary/AFP via Getty Images

2018

Dec

Shutdown

Read more Trump and Mike Pence meet the Democratic House speaker-designate, Nancy Pelosi, and Senate minority leader, Chuck Schumer. The talks went nowhere and America endured the longest government shutdown in its history, ending after 34 days when Trump backed down over funding for his border wall. Photograph by Michael Reynolds/EPA

2019

Jan

Nancy’s clapback

Read more Nancy Pelosi offers Trump sarcastic applause at his State of the Union address, generating an instant meme. The House speaker proved a nemesis to Trump throughout his presidency. By October 2020, the two had gone a full year without speaking. Photograph by Doug Mills/The New York Times/Pool via Getty Images

2019

Jan

Glass ceilings shattered

Read more Female members of Congress, wearing white to honour the suffragist movement, cheer during Trump’s State of the Union address. A record 102 women serve in the House of Representatives, making up 23.4% of the chamber’s voting members, following historic gains in the midterm elections. Photo by Alex Wong/Getty Images

2019

July

Love’s young dream

Read more Trump meets the North Korean leader, Kim Jong-un, at the demilitarised zone separating the two Koreas. Having once called Kim ‘little rocket man’ and threatened to rain ‘fire and fury’ on his country, Trump later claimed they ‘fell in love’ because of Kim’s ‘beautiful letters’. Photograph by Kevin Lamarque/Reuters

2019

July

Mueller time

Read more Robert Mueller prepares to address the House judiciary committee about Russian interference in the 2016 election. The former special counsel’s testimony, much like his long-awaited report, did not exonerate Trump but failed to deliver the clear verdict that many hoped for. Photograph by Jim Lo Scalzo/EPA

2019

Aug

Trumps and Trudeau

Read more Melania Trump kisses Canada’s prime minister, Justin Trudeau, as Trump stands nearby during the family photo with invited guests at the G7 summit in Biarritz, France. Trump has a strained relationship with his neighbour to the north, calling him ‘two-faced’. Photograph by Carlos Barria/Reuters

2019

Aug

El Paso

Read more Miguel de Anda, born and raised in El Paso, Texas, holds a sign that says, ‘Trump not welcome here’ at a protest against the president’s visit following a mass shooting that left 23 people dead. Trump has embraced the second amendment and National Rifle Association. Photograph by Carlos Barria/Reuters

2019

Nov

Impeaching a president

Read more A day after Mueller’s congressional testimony, Trump called Volodymyr Zelensky, the president of Ukraine, and sought political favours in return for military aid. Fiona Hill, a British-born former national security aide and Russia expert, testifies during impeachment hearings on Capitol Hill. Photograph by J. Scott Applewhite/AP

2019

Nov

Quid pro quo

Read more Trump’s big handwriting is visible on notes when he speaks to the media before departing the White House. He denies offering Ukraine a quid pro quo and insists it was a ‘perfect call’ with Zelenskiy. Republicans, as so often during his presidency, remain loyal and defend him. Photo by Mark Wilson/Getty Images

2020

Jan

Killing Soleimani

Read more Trump orders the killing of Qassem Suleimani in a US drone strike in Baghdad. Suleimani was widely considered to be the most powerful man in Iran after the supreme leader, Ayatollah Ali Khamenei. Trump departs the grand foyer of the White House after announcing that no Americans were killed or wounded when Iran launched more than a dozen ballistic missiles against two military bases in Iraq. Photo by Jabin Botsford/The Washington Post via Getty Images

2020

Apr

The coronavirus changes everything

Read more Trump is warned by White House officials in January that the coronavirus poses the ‘biggest national security threat’ of his presidency, according to journalist Bob Woodward, but downplays it for months. He holds coronavirus taskforce briefings with Anthony Fauci and other experts but wonders aloud if injecting disinfectant could provide a cure. Photo by Jabin Botsford/The Washington Post via Getty Images

2020

Jun

Build that wall

Read more Trump takes part in a ceremony commemorating the 200th mile of border wall at the US border with Mexico in San Luis, Arizona. Trump made the wall a central theme of his 2016 election campaign. The supreme court is to review a dispute over his diversion of military funds to build it. Photo by Saul Loeb/AFP via Getty Images

2020

Jun

The comeback falls flat

Read more Supporters listen as Lara Trump speaks before Trump’s arrival for a Make America Great Again rally in Tulsa, Oklahoma, in June. But the arena is at least one-third empty and there is little evidence of face masks or physical distancing. Herman Cain, who attends, dies from Covid-19 the following month. Photo by Jabin Botsford/The Washington Post via Getty Images

2020

Jun

Holy war

Read more Trump holds a Bible outside St John’s Church near the White House after park police and national guard troops fire teargas and chase peaceful protesters from Lafayette Square during Black Lives Matters protests sparked by the police killing of George Floyd in Minneapolis. Minutes earlier Trump says: ‘I am your president of law and order.’ Photograph by BRENDAN SMIALOWSKI/AFP via Getty Images

2020

Oct

Patient Zero

Read more Trump walks from the Marine One helicopter to the White House as he returns from Bedminster, New Jersey, where he held a campaign fundraiser. He soon announces that he and his wife, Melania, tested positive for the coronavirus. He spends three nights at a military hospital but returns boasting that he feels like ‘Superman’. Photograph by Carolyn Kaster/AP
